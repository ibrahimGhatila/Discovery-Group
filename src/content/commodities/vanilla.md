Ugandan vanilla benefits from two harvests a year — a rhythm shared
only with parts of Indonesia and almost nowhere else in Africa.
Beans are hand-pollinated by the cooperative's growers between
September and November (and again in April), reaching the curing
floor green and full-bodied.

The four-stage cure — kill, sweat, dry, condition — is the difference
between commodity vanilla and gourmet vanilla. We use shorter, hotter
killing baths than the Madagascar standard to lock in the
characteristic Ugandan smoke note, then condition in cedar boxes for
a minimum of 90 days before grading.

Available in 1 kg vacuum-sealed retail packs and 5 kg bulk fibreboard.
