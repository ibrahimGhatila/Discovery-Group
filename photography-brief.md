# Photography brief — Discovery Group rebuild

Every photograph on the live site should be commissioned, original, and
shot at one of Discovery's origin locations or at the Namanve facility.
No stock. No iPhone snapshots. The brief below is the shotlist needed
to ship the redesigned site, organised by where each image goes.

## Direction

### Mood

- **Documentary, not promotional.** Catalogue what's happening, don't
  stage it for the camera. The site should feel like a quiet observer
  of real work.
- **Natural light first.** No on-camera flash. If a fill is needed,
  bounce off a white card or use a single off-camera strobe diffused
  to neutral.
- **Warm but accurate.** Hold the white balance close to native; don't
  push to "golden hour" filters. Ugandan red clay is already red — let
  it be.
- **People at work, not portraits to camera.** Workers should be
  doing what they are paid to do, not posing. Eye contact with the
  camera only in named producer / leadership portraits.

### Composition

- **Wide shots establish context** — drying beds at first light, the
  Namanve dry-mill outfeed, a mountainside in haze.
- **Mid shots show process** — hands on the cherry selection table,
  the cascade ferment turning, a moisture probe in vanilla.
- **Detail shots punctuate** — a single cherry, a cocoa cut-test, a
  Robusta screen tray with overflow beans.
- **Aspect ratios used on the site:**
  - `4/5` portrait — producer profiles, half-page heroes
  - `3/2` landscape — Orocafe product card
  - `16/9` landscape — wide story spreads
  - `1/1` — index thumbnails

### What to avoid

- Hard gradient overlays — typography on photo should sit in negative
  space, not on top of a darkened image.
- Posed group shots in front of branded backdrops.
- Hands of an obviously different demographic to the location's
  actual workforce — composite shots from other origins are not
  acceptable.
- Drone aerials of generic green hills with no context — landscape
  shots must be specific to the relevant region.

### Consent and rights

Every named producer, cooperative member, or employee whose face
appears on the site must sign a release. The release covers website
use, marketing collateral, and trade-show display. The signed releases
should be filed with the photographer's invoice and accessible from
the marketing folder for audits.

## Deliverables expected from the photographer

For each commissioned image:

1. **High-res RAW** archived to client storage
2. **Web-optimised JPG and WebP** — 2400px on the long edge, sRGB,
   80% quality
3. **AVIF** if the photographer's tooling supports it
4. **Signed releases** for every identifiable person
5. **Location metadata** (district, cooperative if applicable, date)
6. **Caption** matching the agreed shotlist entry

## Production order of operations

1. **Namanve facility shoot first** (4–5 days) — most controlled
   conditions, covers ~40% of the shotlist.
2. **Mt. Elgon trip** (3–4 days during harvest, Oct–Feb) — Arabica
   coffee and chilli simultaneously possible.
3. **Bundibugyo trip** (3 days) — cocoa.
4. **Mukono / Lake Victoria crescent** (1–2 days) — vanilla.
5. **Lira / northern Uganda** (2 days during harvest) — sesame and
   chilli, possibly combined with the Mt. Elgon trip's tail end.

Plan the producer-portrait sittings into each origin trip — don't
return for them separately.

## Budget framing (indicative)

For a Uganda-based specialty photographer with origin experience, a
full shotlist of this scope is typically a 12–15 shoot-day engagement
plus travel and editing. Pricing varies; the brief is dimensioned to
be deliverable in a single contracted block of work rather than
fragmented across vendors. Allocate budget for releases, location
fees where applicable, and post-production.
